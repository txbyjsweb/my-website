[ Waves & Optics ]

Progressive Wave

Transfers energy from a source to the surroundings without net transfer of matter.

Electromagnetic Wave

A Wave that does not require a Medium

Mechanical Wave

A Wave that does require a Medium

Longitudinal Wave

A Longitudinal Wave is an oscillation parallel to the direction of energy transfer

Examples of Longitudinal Waves

  • 1. Sound Waves
  • 2. P Waves

Transverse Waves

Transverse Waves are oscillations perpendicular the the direction of energy transfer

Examples of Transverse Waves

  • 1. Water Waves
  • 2. S Waves
  • 3. Electromagnetic Waves

Polarised Waves

Polarised Waves are oscillations restricted to a single plane perpendicular to the direction of propagation

Unpolarised Waves

Unpolarised Waves are oscillations in a number of planes perpendicular to the direction of propagation

Are Polarised Waves Transverse or Longitudinal?

Only Transverse Waves can be polarised, because only they oscillate in planes perpendicular to the direction of propagation, unlike Longitudinal Waves which oscillate parallel to the direction of propagation

Amplitude of a Wave

Wavelength of a Wave

Two consecutive, identical points on the wave in phase parallel to direction of travel

Frequency of a Wave

Number of Waves passing a point per second

Period of a Wave

Time Taken for one Wavelength to pass a point

Velocity of a Wave

Distance travelled parallel to the wave direction by a wavefront per time unit

Phase Difference between two Points on a Wave

Tells us how much one point leads or lags the other point

Phase Difference between 2 Waves

Tells us how much a point on one wave leads or lags an identical point on the other wave

The Phase

The Phase refers to the position of a point within the cycle of the wave relative to the starting point.

Units of Phases

Radians or Degrees due to similarities of wave cycles and circular motion
