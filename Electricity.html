[ Electricity ]

EMF Definition

The EMF of a source of electricity is defined as the electrical energy produced per unit charge passing through the source (Measured in Volts)

Ohm's Law

The Potential Difference through a metallic conductor is proportional to the current through it, provided physical conditions do not change

Superconductor Definition

A Superconductor is a wire or a device made of a material that has 0 resistivity at and below a critical temperature, depending on the material. When a current passes through, there is no Potential Difference across it because the Resistance is 0, therefore there is no heating effect

Two ways in which you can investigate the IV characteristics of components in a circuit are

A Variable Resistor to vary Current or A Potential Divider

Circuit Rules

  • 1. At any junction in a circuit, the Total Current leaving the junction is equal to the Total Current entering.
  • 2. The Current Entering a Component is the same Leaving a Component
  • 3. The Current Passing through 2 or more components in series is the same through each component
